Critical Finding: Limited Predictive Power

‚ö†Ô∏è All Models Show Near-Zero or Negative R¬≤ Scores

Best Model R¬≤ = 0.0131 (test set) and -0.0203 (cross-validation)

What Does This Mean?

  • R¬≤ ‚âà 0: The model explains almost none of the variance in the target variable
  • Negative R¬≤: The model performs worse than simply predicting the mean
  • Implication: Using raw cryptocurrency prices as features provides minimal predictive value

Why Is This Happening?

  • Market Efficiency: Cryptocurrency markets may be semi-efficient, making price-based prediction difficult
  • High Volatility: Crypto prices exhibit extreme volatility and noise
  • Feature Engineering Gap: Raw prices lack financial context (momentum, volatility, trends, etc.)
  • Multicollinearity: Cryptocurrencies often move together, creating redundant information

Recommendations for Improvement

  • Engineer financial features: returns, moving averages, RSI, Bollinger Bands, momentum indicators
  • Include market microstructure: volume, bid-ask spreads, order book depth
  • Add sentiment data: social media sentiment, news sentiment
  • Consider time-series specific models: ARIMA, LSTM, Prophet

üí° Why the Disagreement?

  • Built-in importance: Measures how often a feature is used for splitting (frequency-based)
  • SHAP importance: Measures actual contribution to predictions, accounting for all possible feature interactions
  • Multicollinearity: When cryptos move together (high correlation), built-in methods may arbitrarily favor one over another
  • Conditional effects: A feature may be important only in specific contexts (interactions), which SHAP captures better
Practical Implication: Trust SHAP importance over built-in importance when features are correlated, especially in financial markets where assets often move together.
